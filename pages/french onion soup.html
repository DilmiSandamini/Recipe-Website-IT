French Onion Soup

Ingredients:

For French Onion Soup:

  • 4 large yellow onions (thinly sliced)
  • 3 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 tbsp all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine
  • 6 cups beef broth (or a mix of beef & chicken)
  • 1 tsp dried thyme (or 2 sprigs fresh thyme)
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • Baguette slices (toasted)
  • 1 1/2 cups shredded Gruyère cheese
  • Fresh parsley or thyme (optional garnish)

Directions:

For French Onion Soup:

  • Melt butter in a large pot over medium heat.
  • Add sliced onions and cook slowly, stirring often, until deeply caramelized (about 40 minutes).
  • Add garlic and cook for 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
  • Stir in flour and cook for 1 minute to thicken.
  • Deglaze the pot with white wine, scraping up the brown bits.
  • Add beef broth, thyme, bay leaf, salt, and pepper. Simmer for 20–25 minutes.
  • Remove bay leaf and discard.
  • Preheat the oven broiler. Ladle soup into oven-safe bowls.
  • Top each bowl with a toasted baguette slice and generous amount of shredded Gruyère cheese.
  • Place bowls under the broiler until cheese is melted and bubbly (about 2–3 minutes).
  • Carefully remove from oven and serve hot with a sprinkle of fresh thyme or parsley (optional).
