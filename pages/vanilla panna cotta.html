Vanilla Panna Cotta

Ingredients:

For Vanilla Panna Cotta:

  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup whole milk
  • 1/3 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 1/2 tsp unflavored gelatin
  • 1 1/2 tsp vanilla extract (or 1/2 vanilla bean, seeds scraped)
  • Fresh berries or berry compote (for topping)
  • Optional: honey or mint leaves for garnish

For Vanilla Panna Cotta :

  • 120g semi-sweet chocolate (chopped)
  • 100g unsalted butter
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 egg yolks
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • Pinch of salt
  • 2 tbsp all-purpose flour

Directions:

For Vanilla Panna Cotta:

  • In a small bowl, sprinkle the gelatin over cold milk. Let it bloom for 5–10 minutes until soft.
  • In a saucepan, combine the heavy cream, sugar, and vanilla extract (or seeds from vanilla bean).
  • Heat the cream mixture over medium heat until it’s hot but not boiling.
  • Stir occasionally to dissolve the sugar.
  • Once the cream is hot, remove from heat and whisk in the softened gelatin mixture until fully dissolved.
  • Let the mixture cool slightly for 5 minutes.
  • Pour the panna cotta mixture into lightly greased ramekins or dessert cups.
  • Cover each with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 4–6 hours, or until fully set.
  • To serve, run a knife around the edges of the ramekin and gently unmold onto a plate (optional).
  • Top with fresh berries, berry compote, or a drizzle of honey for extra flavor and presentation.
